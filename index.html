Summer Camp

This summer, we welcomed over 850 kids to our 400-acre Star Lake Camp, an outdoor oasis just one hour from the heart of Manhattan.

In addition to activities like hiking, swimming, arts and crafts, archery, and music classes, our campers enjoyed a special visit during Sports Week from New York Giants alumni Keith Elias and Roman Oben, as well as Minnesota Timberwolves co-owner Marc Lore.

They brought a motivating message of persistence and perseverance to campers, and how continual drive can propel kids forward to excel in their careers.

We Scream for Ice Cream

Our Newburgh location introduced their brand new Ice Cream cart this summer with frequent trips out during the week to offer free ice cream to children and adults in the community.

This presented an opportunity to get to know our neighbors and to share valuable information about the programs and services available out of our community and worship center.

Cooling Centers

As part of a partnership with New York City Emergency Management, The Salvation Army opened many of its city locations as cooling centers during active heat advisories this summer.

Zachary Hodgson, our Director of Emergency Services, encouraged New Yorkers to check on their vulnerable neighbors during high temperatures and to stop at one of our cooling centers for a snack, cold drink, and seat in the air conditioning.

Back-to-School

On August 10, New York Cares partnered with The Salvation Army to pack 8,000 backpacks to be distributed at our 38 local community centers.

We know these gifts of school supplies will help to ease the financial burden placed on families and provide the necessary tools young students need to thrive in the upcoming school year.

Maritza's Story

After travelling to the United States in hopes of securing a better situation for her family, Maritza and her children were met with continual roadblocks.

When she turned to The Salvation Army for help, she was given the support that she needed to find housing, a job, and a safe place for her children to thrive. Through the toughest moments, faith and a sense of family encouraged Maritza to persevere.

Benefit Concert featuring Andy Grammer

On October 6th, 2022 The Salvation Army Greater New York Division will host an intimate acoustic benefit concert featuring multi-platinum singer and songwriter Andy Grammer.

Proceeds from the concert will fund efforts to combat food insecurity and homelessness in our area.

For more information about the concert, please visit salvationarmyny.org/concert
